About the PCB Sentinel System

What We Do

The PCB Sentinel System is a special project that cleans the Los Angeles River by removing dangerous chemicals called PCBs. Think of PCBs like invisible pollution that harms fish, plants, and people who use the river.

PCBs are man-made chemicals that were used in things like electrical equipment and building materials from the 1920s until 1979. Even though they were banned over 40 years ago, these chemicals still stick around in the water and soil. They don't break down easily, which means they keep causing problems for a long time.

Our system uses special filters and sensors to find and catch these harmful chemicals before they can hurt the river's animals and plants. We're working to make the LA River clean and safe for everyone in our community.

Protecting Our River

Our Goals & Mission

We have clear targets to make the LA River healthy again. Here's what we're working toward:

Remove Dangerous PCBs

Our biggest goal is to reduce PCB levels by 85% over the next five years. Right now, the river has too much PCB pollution, which makes it unsafe for fish and people.

We want to bring PCB levels down to what scientists call "safe environmental standards." This means the water will be clean enough that fish can live healthy lives and people can safely enjoy activities near the river.

Use Smart Technology

We use high-tech sensors and filters that work 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. These sensors can detect even tiny amounts of PCBs in the water, kind of like how a smoke detector can smell smoke before you do.

Our filtration system then captures the PCBs and removes them safely. The technology constantly learns and adjusts to work better over time, making sure we catch as many harmful chemicals as possible.

Bring Nature Back

When PCB levels drop, fish, birds, plants, and other wildlife can return to the river. We want to create a healthy ecosystem where native species can thrive again.

Our long-term vision is to have a river where families can fish, kayak, and enjoy nature safely. We also want to see more parks and green spaces along the riverbank that everyone can use.

Why This Matters

The LA River flows through 51 miles of Southern California and affects over 10 million people. When the river is polluted with PCBs, it doesn't just hurt the fish - it affects everyone who lives near it.

PCBs can cause serious health problems in humans, including cancer and damage to the immune system. By cleaning the river, we're protecting the health of our entire community and making sure future generations have a clean, safe river to enjoy.

How Our Technology Works

Advanced Filtration System

A Three-Step Process

Our PCB Sentinel System uses a simple but powerful three-step process to clean the river:

1

Detect the Problem

Our sensors constantly test the water, measuring PCB levels every few minutes. They can find even very small amounts of pollution that human eyes can't see.

2

Filter and Capture

Special filters trap PCB particles as water flows through the system. Think of it like a really good coffee filter that only catches the bad stuff and lets clean water pass through.

3

Safely Remove

We carefully collect the captured PCBs and send them to special facilities that can destroy them safely. This follows strict environmental rules to make sure the pollution doesn't end up somewhere else.

Benefits for Everyone

  • Safer Parks and Recreation: People can enjoy walking, biking, and playing near the river without worrying about pollution

  • More Wildlife: Birds, turtles, and other animals will come back to make their homes along the river
  • Learning Opportunities: Students can visit the river for science projects and learn about environmental protection
  • Community Pride: A cleaner river makes our neighborhoods more attractive and increases property values
