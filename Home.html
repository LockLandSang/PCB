About the PCB Sentinel System

Our Mission

The PCB Sentinel System is a pioneering initiative aimed at cleaning the Los Angeles River by removing harmful PCBs (Polychlorinated Biphenyls). Our project combines cutting-edge technology with environmental stewardship to address one of the region's most pressing ecological challenges.

PCBs are toxic industrial compounds that were widely used in electrical equipment until they were banned in the 1970s. Despite the ban, these persistent pollutants continue to contaminate waterways, posing serious risks to aquatic life and human health.

Our system deploys advanced filtration and monitoring technologies to detect, capture, and safely dispose of PCBs from the river water, creating a cleaner, safer environment for all.

Project Goals & Objectives

Eliminate PCB Contamination

Our primary goal is to eliminate PCB contamination from the LA River, improving water quality and supporting aquatic life. We're committed to reducing PCB levels to safe environmental standards.

Advanced Technology Deployment

We utilize state-of-the-art sensors and advanced filtration systems to detect and remove PCBs efficiently. Our technology continuously monitors water quality and adapts to changing conditions.

Restore Ecosystem Health

By reducing PCB levels, we aim to restore the river's ecosystem, enhancing biodiversity and providing a safer environment for recreation. Our long-term vision includes a thriving river ecosystem.

Our Technology

PCB Detection & Removal System

Innovative Solutions

Our PCB Sentinel System employs a multi-stage approach to detect and remove PCBs from the river water:

1

Advanced Sensors

Real-time monitoring of PCB concentrations using specialized detection equipment.

2

Filtration Units

High-efficiency filters that capture PCB particles from water flow.

3

Safe Disposal

Proper containment and disposal of captured PCBs according to environmental regulations.

Project Timeline

Project Initiation

2020

Research and development phase began with environmental scientists and engineers.

Pilot Program Launch

2021

Initial deployment of PCB detection systems in a 2-mile river segment.

Full-Scale Implementation

2022

Expansion to cover 12 miles of the LA River with advanced filtration systems.
